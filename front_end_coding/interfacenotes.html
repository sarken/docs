Work Blurb

Work blurb has been added to significantly over time. I don't think some of the additions are effective or good design. The code structure is not robust enough to incorporate the changes and has lost its vertical scanlines, which means it's harder to read. The extra labelling overwhlems the unique metadata. The wider definition terms give the blurb a fixed width of well over 10em; on a page with a dashboard and/or filters, this adds up to a page that does not flow into small screen sizes well, which is a primary layout design goal.

Navigation

Navigation lacks consistency, coherency, and has invaded several European countries.

I've combined as many blocks as I can without good ruby knowledge, and commented the partials with what more needs doing. I've styled all navigation, buttons, and actions consistently (I may have missed a couple) and marked up every partial with regions so back end know where to put new links/actions.

I've consistently styled "current" as reversed: white on black. This is now consistent across navigation, main nav, dashboard, and pagination.

Home Page

The home page is currently, imo, incoherent. The header/masthead particularly, is poor. Mast heads chiefly operate to provde a strong framing identity across the site, so to have a completely different one on one page, the home page even, is a bad decision.

I suggest that the primary audience for the home page is the new user. Its purpose therefore should be to (1) identify the site with logo, livery, and short description (2) invite the user to join up and stay (3) demonstrate the site is "live" - full of new content and other users.

It achieves some of these goals already. I proposed an ad style frame on the home page, literally packaging the site with the logo and inviting the user to come "inside". Elz does not like this design.

Home page could reuse a normal page and stick it in an ident:

<div id="ident">
	<p>Be part of our open beta</p>
	<h3><a href="">Sign Up Now</a></h3>
	<h3>The Archive of Our Own</h3>
	<p>is a fan-created, fan-run, non-profit, 
	noncommercial archive for transformative fanworks.</p>
</div>
<div id="home">
	normal page here
</div>

body {background:#222 url (logo.png) top right no-repeat; color:#fffef9;} #home {width:70%; float:left; margin:1em} #ident {width:20%; float:right; margin: 200px 1em 1em 1em}

Once logged in, the sign up frame disappears and you just have a normal page. Maybe a transition on the sign up, so when clicked the main page expands on a slide, to give the feeling of entering the site, and for wow factor etc. Roughly:
